CHAPTER ONE
Understanding Value Education
Expected Outcomes:
The students would be able to learn about 
* Need of Value Education
* Basic Guidelines for Value Education
* The Content of Value Education

1.1 Need for Value Education
All human beings aspire continuously for a happy life, a fulfilling and successful life, and the purpose of education is to provide adequate competence to actualize this aspiration. For this, first of all it is essential to understand- what is really VALUABLE for human being, and, what is really conducive to a happy and fulfilling life? - this is the VALUE domain. Then, it is essential to know- How to actualize it? How to make it happen? – this is the domain of ‘SKILLS’. Hence there is an essential complementarity between ‘VALUES’ and ‘SKILLS’ for the success of any human endeavor.
The subject which enables us to understand ‘What is Valuable’ for human happiness is called Value Education (VE).
Thus, VE enables us to understand our needs and visualize our goals correctly. Also, it indicates the direction for their fulfillment. It also helps to remove our confusions and contradictions and bring harmony at all the levels.
The present education system has become largely skill-based with prime emphasis is on science and technology. However, science and technology can only help to provide the means to achieve what is considered valuable. It is NOT within the scope of science and technology to provide the competence of deciding the thing which is really valuable.
VE is a crucial missing link in the present education system. Because of this deficiency, most of our efforts may prove to be counterproductive. Serious crises at the level of individual, society and environment are manifesting. Hence, the need to rectify this situation is strongly realized by the people involved in imparting holistic and comprehensive education to the students.

1.2 Basic Guidelines for Value Education
In order to qualify for Value Education, a holistic and comprehensive education system should comply with the following guidelines.
1. Universal: It needs to be applicable to all human beings irrespective of cast, creed, nationality, religion, etc. for all the times and places.
2. Rational: It has to appeal to human reasoning.
3. Natural and verifiable: It has to be naturally acceptable to the human being who goes through the course and there needs to be every provision in nature for its fulfillment. It needs to be verifiable and experientially verifiable, and not based on dogmas, beliefs or assumptions.
4. All encompassing: It needs to be cover all dimensions (realization, thought, behavior and work) & levels (individual, family, society, nature & existence) of human life and profession.
5. Leading to harmony: The Value Education ultimately is targeted to promote harmony within the individual, harmony among human beings and harmony with nature.

1.3 Content of Value Education
Value of any unit in this existence is its participation in the larger order of which it is a part. eg. Value of a pen is that it can write. Here writing is the participation of the pen in the larger order which includes pen, paper and human being. Value of an eye is that it can be used for seeing. Value of a spinach plant is that it gives nutrition to animals and human beings. 
Now the question is about the value of a human being. This question enquires about the participation of a human being in the bigger order. The bigger order includes human beings and rest of existence (plants, air, water, soil, animals, birds etc). The value of a human being is the participation of human being to ensure the continuity of harmony in that order. In order to understand human values, we need to study human being along with all that is there in the existence, and the role of human being in the relationship with each and every unit in the entire existence.

1.4 Scope of study
The course on value education must include all the dimensions of living (thought, behavior, work & realization) at all the four levels of human living (individual, family, society, nature & existence). 
Accordingly, the content of Value Education will enable the individual to understand about myself, my aspirations, my happiness and understand the other entities in existence vis-à-vis the human being, understand the goal of human life comprehensively, understand the innate interconnectedness, order, the co-existence, the self-regulation and the cycle in the nature. It should encompass harmony at all the four levels, namely individual, family, society and nature & existence. Finally, it is to learn to live in accordance with this understanding by being vigilant to one’s thought, behavior and work.

1.5 Process of Value Education
The process of value education is to be ascertained before we proceed. Let us now acquaint ourselves with the process of value education which we are going to adopt. In this course, various aspects of reality facilitating the understanding of human values will be as proposals. You need to verify these proposals for yourself and examine your living in this light. Let us see how we can verify these proposals.
* We will verify these proposals through self-exploration. We shall investigate into these proposals and try to verify within us on our own right.
* This self-exploration will be done on the basis of whether the proposals are acceptable to us in a natural manner. They need to be naturally acceptable to us and not just imposed externally.
* Self-exploration will also include verifying the proposals through experiential validation, i.e. by living according to them. Experiential validation will ascertain that when we live our life on the basis of this education, our living will be fulfilling to us as well as our surroundings.
* Since the process is of self-exploration, and not of giving sermons or prescribing do’s and don’ts, you need not agree to all that is said, but only be ready to investigate into them sincerely. We don’t want to just theorize and impose stated truths.
* This process of self-exploration has to be in the form of a dialogue, a dialogue between the presenter and the receivers to begin with; and slowly to translate into a dialogue within the receiver himself/herself. Each one of us can conduct this verification within ourselves.

CHAPTER TWO
Self-Exploration: The Process for Value Education
Expected Outcome:

2.1 Self-Exploration and its Purpose
1. It is a process of dialogue between ‘what you are’ and ‘what you really want to be’. This process helps in focusing the attention on self (I), your present beliefs and aspirations vis-à-vis what you really want to be (what is naturally acceptable to you). It is a process of discovering that there is something innate, invariant and universal in all human beings. This enables us to look at our confusions and contradictions within and resolve them by becoming aware of our natural acceptance.
2. It is a process of self-evolution through self-investigation which successively enables the explorer to evolve by bridging the above gap between ‘what you are’ and ‘what you really want to be’.
3. It is a process of knowing oneself and through that knowing entire existence. The exploration starts by asking simple questions about yourself, which gives you clarity about your being, and then slowly gain clarity about everything around you.
4. It is a process of recognizing one’s relation with every unit in existence and fulfilling it. It is a process of becoming aware about your relationship with other entities in existence and through that discovering the interconnectedness, co-existence and order in the entire existence, and living accordingly.
5. It is a process of knowing human conduct, human character and living accordingly. It is a process of discovering the definitiveness of human conduct and human character and enabling one to be definite in thought, behavior and work.
6. This process of self-exploration helps you be in harmony within yourself, and in harmony with everything around (entire existence).
7. It is a process of Swatantrata and Swarajya starting from exploration of Swatva.
* Swatva: Innateness of Self (The natural acceptance).
* Swatantrata: Self organized (Being in harmony in oneself)
* Swarajya: Self Expression, Self-Extension (living in harmony with others and thus participation towards harmony in the entire existence).
Swatva→ Swatantrata→ Swarajya
The Swatva is intact in each and every individual. Just by being in dialogue with it, we attain Swatantrata enabling us to work for Swarajya.

2.2 Content of Self Exploration
The main focus of self-exploration is myself- the Human Being. Basically, it should dwell on the following two key questions.
* What do I really want in life, or what is the goal of human life?
* How to fulfil it? What is the program to actualize the above?
In brief, the above two questions cover the whole domain of human aspirations and human endeavor. Thus, it forms the content of self-exploration.

2.3 Process of Self Exploration
a. whatever is being presented is a PROPOSAL.
b. Don’t assume it to be true
c. Verify at your own right (on the basis of natural acceptance)
* Not on the basis of scriptures
* Not on the basis of equipment/ instrument
* Not on the basis of the input from other humans.

2.4 Natural Acceptance
1. The natural acceptance does not change with time. It remains invariant with time. This can be easily verified. For example, our acceptance for trust or respect does not change with age. People hundred years ago also had the same natural acceptance. We can try to verify this within our span of observation.
2. It does not depend on the place. Whether we are in New Delhi, New York or Abu Dhabi, if we address our natural acceptance, the answer would everywhere be the same. 
3. It does not depend on our beliefs or past conditionings.
4. The natural acceptance is constantly present in every human being
5. Natural acceptance is the same for all.

3.1 Continuous Happiness and Prosperity (The Basic Human Aspirations)
Happiness: The state or situation in which the individual live in harmony with the system is termed as happiness. The individual like to remain in that state or situation. In other words, we can say “To be in a state of liking is happiness” and “To be in harmony is Happiness”.
Unhappiness: The state of being in contradiction/disharmony is termed as unhappiness. No one likes to be in such state.
Prosperity: It is the feeling of having or making available more than required physical facilities. Following two things are essential to ensure the feeling of prosperity.
* Correct assessment of the need for physical facilities.
* The competence of making available more than required physical facilities (through production).
Wealth: It is a physical thing in terms of accumulated money, property or having a lot of physical facilities.

3.2 Finding Basic Aspirations
Make a list of all that you want in your life and reasons for wanting them. For example, when you start listing, your list may comprise of the following things
* to be an engineer or an MBA professional
* to get the highest grade
* to be the first ranker in the examination
* to be the smartest guy in the crowd
* to be the master of all trades
* to earn more wealth than the wealthiest person in the world
After making the list check if there is anything basic to all of these. Check the presence of any basic aspiration of yours behind all your wants. This is an exercise worth doing to find out your basic aspiration.
We can examine that behind the desire to get the first rank, there is something more basic desire. Why do I want to get the first rank? If you keep asking this question, you will find that there is a basic desire and that is to be happy.
Desire: Continuous Happiness and Prosperity
Verify whether you want to be happy or unhappy.
Verify whether you want to be prosperous or deprived.

3.3 Exploring Happiness and Prosperity
let us have a critical look at the prevailing notions of happiness and prosperity while keeping the above discussion in mind. We are generally trying to achieve happiness and prosperity by maximizing accumulation and consumption of physical facilities in current scenario. This is an attempt to achieve happiness through sensory pleasures. In place of the requirement for fulfilling bodily needs, the physical facilities are considered as the means of maximizing happiness, This results in wrong assessment of wants for physical facilities as being unlimited. This pursuit is self-defeating because it amounts to try to fulfil unlimited wants through limited resources. Neither can we expect to achieve continuous happiness through sensory pleasures nor can we have the feeling of prosperity. This effort is causing problems at all the levels. It is becoming anti-ecological and anti-people, and threatening the human survival itself. Some of the consequences of such a trend are summarized below:
At the level of the individual: Rise in the problems of depression, psychological disorders, suicides, stress, insecurity, psychosomatic diseases, loneliness etc.
At the level of the family: Breaking of joint families, mistrust, conflict between older and younger generations, insecurity in relationships, divorce, dowry tortures, family feuds, wasteful expenditure in family functions etc.
At the level of the Society: Growing incidences of terrorism and naxalism, rising communalism, spreading castecism, racial and ethnic struggle, wars between nations, attempts of genocide, fear of nuclear and genetic warfare etc.
At the level of nature: Global warming, water pollution/depletion, air pollution, noise pollution, resource depletion (minerals and mineral oils), sizeable deforestations, loss of fertility of soil etc.

3.4 Some misconceptions about happiness
* I will be bored of happiness if I am always happy.
* I will grow only if I am unhappy. If I become happy, my growth will stop.
* I need to be unhappy to realize happiness.
* We think of others only when we are unhappy. Thus it is important/useful to be unhappy so that one can help others.
* Happiness and unhappiness go together; they cannot be separated.
* Yes, I want happiness. But my desiring does not guarantee it. So, why to talk of the desire?
* My happiness depends on the others. What can I do about it?
* We do not want happiness for ourselves – but we want to make others happy (while we stay unhappy).

CHAPTER FOUR
The Program to Fulfil Basic Human Aspirations
Expected Outcomes:
The student will be able to learn
* Basic requirements for fulfilment of Human Aspirations
* Difference between Animal and Human consciousness.

4.1 The basic requirements for fulfilment of aspirations:
It is prevailing notion that the fulfilment of the aspirations is possible by physical facilities and for want of this we keep accumulating the physical facilities. It could be seen that the physical facilities are required to human being but these cannot fulfil the human aspirations alone. The human being need the feeling of relationship along with physical facilities to fulfil the aspirations. On the other hand, the animals need the physical facilities which are sufficient for them. No other thing is necessary for them to be satisfied. We can analyze the basic requirements.
* One requirement for fulfilment of aspiration of every human being is physical facilities which includes food, clothing, shelter and gadgets/instruments etc. We can analyze whether this is all that we want or we need something more than this. 
* Other than physical facilities, I want the feeling of relationship. The feeling of relationship is an important issue while behaving with other human beings.
* We need to examine the cause of miseries in our lives whether it is due to lack of physical facilities or due to lack of feelings in relationship. Explore this on the level of your family first.
* Next, we need to explore the focus of my efforts. Whether it is on earning of physical facilities or in ensuring relationship. This is a common fallacy in our lives that a large part of our problems are due to lack of relationship while our effort and attention is focused on earning physical facilities most of the time.
* Examine the need of understanding (about the relationships and physical facilities)
* We can find that there is the need of Right Understanding, Relationship and Physical Facilities for fulfilling the aspirations of human beings.

4.2 The correct priority:
In order to decide the correct priority of the requirements, check the role of Right Understanding while deciding the behavior with other human beings and deciding the requirement of physical facilities. It could be seen that the right understanding is necessary to ensure right Feelings in relationships and assess the need of physical facilities correctly. Thus, it could be concluded that the Right Understanding is supposed to be on first priority. The Relationship may be considered on second priority because working with Right Understanding and right feelings in relationship, the need for physical facilities can be easily accessed and fulfilled. Thus Physical Facilities is on the third priority.

We should understand that we need all the three for fulfilment of our aspirations.
* For animal physical facility is necessary as well as complete whereas for human beings it is necessary but not complete.
* Working only for physical facilities is termed as “living with Animal Consciousness”.
* Working for right understanding as the first priority followed by relationship and then physical facilities is termed as “living with Human Consciousness”.

There is need for transformation from Animal Consciousness to Human Consciousness. It can be accomplished only by working for right understanding as the first priority.
If we look around, we find most of the people in the following two categories:
1. SVDD (Sadhan Viheen Dukhi Daridra)
2. SSDD (Sadhan Sampann Dukhi Daridra)
While the natural acceptance of all human beings is to be
SSSS (Sadhan Sampann Sukhi Samriddha)

4.3 Prevailing notion about happiness and its continuity
A common notion among people is that continuous happiness can be achieved through maximization of pleasant sensory interactions. Explore the truthfulness of the notion by verifying at the level of your natural acceptance. Also, investigate it by studying the consequences of such a notion in the present scenario. Is it possible to ensure continuity of happiness through sensory interactions and through maximization of pleasant interactions?

4.4 Prevailing notion about prosperity
The prevailing notion about prosperity seems to be maximization of physical facilities and struggling to meet unlimited wants with limited resources. We can examine the notion by finding the answers of following questions
* Is it ever possible to have a feeling of being prosperous with the above notion? 
* Is it possible to maintain harmony with Nature or to maintain ecological balance?
* What will happen If the above balance is increasingly disturbed, can human survival be sustained on this planet?
